Two Listeners

Does similarity between two listeners have to come from musical taste, or can it also be found in listening patterns, routines and how strongly each person is connected to music?

Dashboard 1: Taste Profile and Shared Listening

Genre Family Distribution

Bars show what percent of each person's minutes goes to each genre family. Click a family to filter the artist lists.

Top Artist Concentration

Lollipops rank artists by total minutes. A ring around a dot means the artist is in the shared set.
Click an artist to see its details. Its genre family gets outlined in the chart above and the artist is highlighted in the shared list below.

Minutes-Weighted Shared Listening

Each strip splits the total listening minutes into shared and non-shared artists. An artist counts as shared if both of us played it for at least 3 minutes in the selected period.

Dashboard 2: Routine and Listening Intensity

Monthly Listening Volume

Orr is plotted above the baseline and Roman is plotted below it.

Listening Activity by Weekday and Hour

Cells count plays by weekday and hour. Each listener has their own color scale, so you can compare the shape of the routines even though the amounts are very different.
Click a cell to see the totals and top artists for that hour.

Cumulative Artist Discovery

Lines count how many different artists each listener has heard up to each month. A steep line means always finding new music, a flat line means going back to the same artists.

Before Uni vs University: Same Direction or Opposite?

Each mini chart connects the value before university to the value during university, one line per listener. Parallel lines mean we changed in the same way, crossing lines mean we went in opposite directions. Picking a period highlights that side of every chart.
