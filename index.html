Two listeners, one question

Or (Apple Music) and Roman (Spotify) share almost no musical taste (~0.6% time-weighted overlap, 0 shared top-100 artists). Does similarity between listeners have to live in taste — or can it live in listening patterns, routine, and intensity of connection to music?

Side A Dashboard 1 — Two rhythms

Routine and intensity: when and how heavily each person listens.

Intensity river

How does each person's monthly listening volume rise and fall across life periods — and when do they converge or diverge?

Mapping: x = month, height = hours listened that month (Or drawn up, Roman drawn down from a flat shared baseline); color = person. Interactions: hover shows a guide line with both people's hours; the timeline strip below is the period selector (click a band, or "All" above); months outside the selected period are dimmed. The data window starts 2020-01; each area starts at that person's first active month inside it (Roman: 2020-04), not at zero.

Routine heatmap

When in the day and week does each person listen? (Or in the morning, Roman at night.)

Mapping: column = hour of day (local), row = weekday (Sun–Sat), cell darkness = % of that person's plays in the selected period (single-hue ramp per person on a square-root scale — see the legend under each grid — with one shared max so shapes are comparable despite different volumes; grey = no plays at all). Interactions: filtered by the period selector; hover a cell for the exact % and raw play count; hovering any cell highlights that weekday row in both heatmaps.

Day-length distribution

On days someone actually listens, how heavy is a typical day — and who has the much heavier ones?

Mapping: x = length of an active listening day (fixed buckets), bar height = % of that person's active days in the selected period; color = person. One annotation calls out the raw 5h+ day counts, where magnitude itself is the finding. Interactions: filtered by the period selector; hover a bar for the exact day count.

Side B Dashboard 2 — Depth of connection

A "Wrapped-plus" view: one plain question per chart. The two linked charts below are two halves of one story — discovering artists, and those artists sticking. All filtered by the same period selector.

Do you keep discovering, or stay in a known world?

How many artists did each person hear for the first time, year by year?

Mapping: x = year, y = artists first played that year (line per person, color = person). Years inside the selected period are emphasized, the rest dimmed. Honesty: each history starts somewhere (Or 2018-09, Roman 2019-09), so the earliest years count returning favorites as "new"; the last year is partial. Interactions: filtered by the period selector; click a year to filter the stickiness chart to that year's cohort (click again to clear); hover for both counts.

Of all the artists you tried, how many stuck?

Each person's artist list, split by how many plays an artist ever got: one-night stands vs. lasting relationships.

Mapping: one 100%-wide bar per person; segment length = share of that person's artists by all-time play count (1 play / 2–9 / 10–49 / 50+); darker = more plays = stuck, in lightness steps of that person's single hue. Play counts come from the full export, so recent discoveries have had less time to stick. Interactions: follows the period selector (artists discovered in those years; "All" = the full list, incl. pre-2020) and the year clicked in the discovery chart — the label above the bars names the active cohort; hover a segment for exact artist counts.

How much did one artist take over?

How is each person's listening time split between the very top of their artist list and everyone else?

Mapping: one 100%-wide bar per person; segment length = share of that person's hours by artist rank (#1, #2–5, #6–10, everyone else); darker = closer to #1, in lightness steps of that person's single hue. The big number is the #1 artist's share — the headline. Interactions: filtered by the period selector (animated); hover a segment for its exact share and hours (and the artist name, for #1).

Liner notes — honest limits: the analysis window is 2020-01 onward (Or's history from 2018-09 is excluded by design; Roman is effectively active from 2021, so the COVID window is Or-heavier early on); skips use the comparable under-30s measure, never native skip counters; genre data is an external, partial add-on and is not charted here; city-level location exists only for Or, so there is no map.
