Two listeners, one question

Or (Apple Music) and Roman (Spotify) share almost no musical taste (~0.6% time-weighted overlap, 0 shared top-100 artists). Does similarity between listeners have to live in taste — or can it live in listening patterns, routine, and intensity of connection to music?

Dashboard 1 — Two rhythms

Routine and intensity: when and how heavily each person listens.

Intensity river

How does each person's monthly listening volume rise and fall across life periods — and when do they converge or diverge?

Mapping: x = month, height = hours listened that month (Or drawn up, Roman drawn down from a flat shared baseline); color = person. Interactions: hover shows a guide line with both people's hours; the timeline strip below is the period selector (click a band, or "All" above); months outside the selected period are dimmed. Roman's history starts 2019-09 — his area starts there, not at zero.

Routine heatmap

When in the day and week does each person listen? (Or in the morning, Roman at night.)

Mapping: column = hour of day (local), row = weekday (Sun–Sat), cell darkness = % of that person's plays in the selected period (single-hue ramp per person, one shared scale so shapes are comparable despite different volumes). Interactions: filtered by the period selector; hover a cell for the exact % and raw play count; hovering any cell highlights that weekday row in both heatmaps.

Day-length distribution

On days someone actually listens, how heavy is a typical day — and who has the much heavier ones?

Mapping: x = length of an active listening day (fixed buckets), bar height = % of that person's active days in the selected period; color = person. Raw day counts are printed on the heavy buckets (3–5h, 5h+), where magnitude itself is the finding. Interactions: filtered by the period selector; hover a bar for the exact day count.

Dashboard 2 — Depth of connection

A "Wrapped-plus" view: one plain question, one headline number per person, filtered by the same period selector.

How much did one artist take over?

The #1 artist's share of that person's listening hours.

Big number = top artist's % of hours; bar = that share of 100%. Hover the bar for hours. Filtered by period.

When you hit play, did you stay?

Share of plays under 30 seconds — the only skip measure computed identically for both services (native skip counters are not comparable).

Big number = % of plays under 30s; bar = that share of 100%. Hover the bar for play counts. Filtered by period.

How many artists make up half your hours?

Count of artists needed to reach 50% of cumulative listening hours — small = a strong center of gravity.

Big number = artists covering 50% of hours, shown against the person's total unique artists in the period. Filtered by period.

Honest limits: histories overlap 2019–2026 (Or from 2018-09, Roman effectively active from 2021 — the Army window is Or-dominant); skips use the comparable under-30s measure, never native skip counters; genre data is an external, partial add-on and is not charted here; city-level location exists only for Or, so there is no map.
